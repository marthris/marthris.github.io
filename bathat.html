Bat Hat
echolocation - seeing with sound

AHO, fall semester 2017
Individual work
Tangible interaction design using Arduino for prototyping
AHO WORKS nominee for prize for Interact Design
Duration 4 weeks

When first assigned the brief for the curious interactive installations I wanted to look further into how animal senses or see the world. After some research I decided to focus on bats ability to echolocate. By sending out high frequency sound signals bats can use the echo bouncing of objects or surroundings to generate a “vision”.

By making a sound the Bat Hat will open a gap making the person using the hat able to see for a brief second.
